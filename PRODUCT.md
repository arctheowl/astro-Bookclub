# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

20s-30s book club members and prospective members. Primarily browsing on phones, casually, after receiving a link or looking up the club. Occasional desktop visit after a meeting invite. The job: see what books the club has read, get a feel for the club's taste, find out when the next meeting is.

## Product Purpose

A landing page for Fleet Book Club. Exists to surface the club's reading history as a browsable cover gallery, reveal book summaries on tap, and communicate the next meeting date. Success = a visitor feels the warmth of the club and knows exactly what was read.

## Brand Personality

Warm, literary, relaxed. Feels like a good bookshop or a well-loved reading nook, not a startup. Unhurried. Invites browsing.

## Anti-references

None stated. Avoid clinical white tech-product aesthetics and anything that feels like a SaaS dashboard.

## Design Principles

1. Covers do the talking — the book grid is the hero content; chrome should recede.
2. Touch-first — every interaction must feel natural on a phone thumb.
3. Warmth over polish — texture, weight, and color temperature matter more than precision grids.
4. Reveal, don't navigate — summary content expands in place rather than routing away.
5. Unhurried rhythm — spacing and transitions should feel calm, never urgent.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Mobile-first. No stated WCAG level, but sufficient contrast and tap target sizes required.
